02 Quantitative · Survey-Based

Narrative Identity, Cognitive Reappraisal & Interpersonal Victimhood

Post-Graduate Dissertation · n = 194 participants

The Objective

To quantify how individuals "story" their life experiences — and how this correlates with perceived victimhood and cognitive reappraisal processes. How does the narrative we hold about ourselves shape our behaviour toward others?

Methodology

  • Quantitative, observational, survey-based design
  • Sample size: 194 participants
  • Correlation and regression analyses to test predictive power of narrative identity
  • APA-style reporting

Impact

Provided insights into cognitive reappraisal strategies applicable to behavioural intervention design — demonstrating how self-narrative shapes social-psychological outcomes in measurable, testable ways.

Relevance to UX Research Designing a survey study with 194 participants requires the same skills as quantitative UX research: instrument design, sampling, statistical analysis, and translating numbers into a coherent behavioural story. The underlying question — "how does internal narrative drive behaviour?" — is the same question UX researchers ask about users.

03 Qualitative · Interview-Based Publication Pending

Humor in Meme Culture as Emotional Expression

Male Young Adults in India · Lead Researcher

The Problem

Understanding non-traditional emotional outlets in a demographic deeply socialised into rigid male identity norms. How do young men in India use digital humour — specifically memes — as a vehicle for emotional expression they cannot access elsewhere?

The Process

  • Conducted in-depth interviews to surface lived experience
  • Applied Thematic Analysis to identify recurring patterns across transcripts
  • Explored meme culture as a behavioural and emotional proxy
  • Identified emergent themes around male identity and social performance

Key Finding

Memes function as a socially sanctioned container for emotions that cultural norms otherwise suppress — a digital coping mechanism that reveals far more about its users than they consciously express. Findings pending publication.

Relevance to UX Research This is end-to-end qualitative UX research applied to a real user segment: defining a research question, recruiting and interviewing participants, conducting thematic analysis, and synthesising findings into insight. The methods are identical; only the domain differs.

A Transformative Framework for Menopausal Health

Lead Researcher & Author · Private Practice Venture

The Objective

To map the intersection of hormonal health, psychological well-being, and systemic gaps in healthcare and workplace support for women in midlife — and propose an evidence-based framework for a new clinical venture.


Key Research Areas

  • Symptom Mapping: Analysed prevalence of psychological (94% irritability) and cognitive (73% memory issues) symptoms unaddressed in traditional clinical settings

  • Applied Framework: Proposed a four-pillar solution: Education, Personalised Care, Workplace Policy (DEI), and Community (CBT-based peer support)

Skills Demonstrated

  • Structured Deliverable: Produced a full white paper — scoped, researched, written, and presented independently as first author (unpublished)

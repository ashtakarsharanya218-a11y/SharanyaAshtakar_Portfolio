01 Qualitative · Interview-Based Publication Pending

Humor in Meme Culture as Means of Emotional Expression Among Male Young Adults: Exploring Stereotypes

Lead Researcher · Sep 2024

The Question

When a cultural context — specifically, rigid male identity norms — blocks direct emotional expression, where does the behaviour actually go? This study treated meme culture as a non-traditional emotional outlet worth taking seriously.

The Process

  • Conducted in-depth interviews with male young adults
  • Applied Thematic Analysis to identify patterns across transcripts
  • Used MAXQDA for qualitative coding and theme development
  • Explored "meme culture" as a proxy for suppressed emotional expression

What Emerged

Memes function as a socially sanctioned container for emotions that cultural norms otherwise suppress — a digital coping mechanism that reveals far more about its users than they consciously express. The research question itself is a behavioural one: when direct expression is blocked, behaviour finds another route.

03 Quantitative · Mediation Study Completed Apr 2026

From Social Evaluation Sensitivity to Social Capital: The Role of Social Initiation

Post-Graduate Dissertation · PES University · Jan – Apr 2026

124
Participants (aged 18–35)
3
Validated instruments (BFNE, ICQ, PSCS-16)
r = .21*
Social initiation → Social capital (p = .019)

The Question

Does social initiation behaviour explain the relationship between an individual's sensitivity to social evaluation (fear of judgment) and their perceived access to personal social capital? In other words — does anxiety about being judged prevent people from building networks?

Methodology

  • Quantitative, cross-sectional, correlational design
  • BFNE (12 items) — fear of negative evaluation
  • ICQ Initiation Subscale (8 items) — social initiation competence
  • PSCS-16 — perceived personal social capital
  • Pearson's correlation + path-based mediation analysis (jamovi)
  • Shapiro-Wilk normality confirmed; parametric tests used

What the Data Said

  • Social initiation significantly predicted social capital (r = .210, p = .019) ✓
  • Fear of evaluation did not significantly inhibit initiation (p = .063)
  • Mediation model rejected — fear of judgment doesn't directly translate into social isolation
  • Conclusion: what you do matters more than how you feel about doing it
Why this matters for research practice A full end-to-end quantitative study — instrument selection, survey design, data collection (n=124), statistical analysis, and a structured written report with discussion of null findings. Null results handled rigorously are a mark of research maturity. The core question — what drives social behaviour despite internal discomfort — is directly relevant to behavioural intervention design.

Narrative Identity, Cognitive Reappraisal & Interpersonal Victimhood

Post-Graduate Dissertation (Year 1) · PES University · Apr 2025

The Question

How does the way people "story" their own life experiences relate to their tendency toward interpersonal victimhood and their capacity for cognitive reappraisal? Does narrative identity shape how we relate to others?


Methodology

  • Quantitative, survey-based, observational design
  • Sample: 194 participants

Impact

Findings offered insights into cognitive reappraisal strategies — demonstrating how the internal narratives we hold about ourselves drive measurable social-psychological outcomes.
